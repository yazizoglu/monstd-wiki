# Economy Balancing Notes

Gold income, tower costs, and upgrade pricing principles.

---

## Income Sources

| Source | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Enemy kill | 1–5 gold | Scales with enemy tier |
| Wave completion bonus | 15–40 gold | Scales with wave number |
| Sell tower | 60% of total cost | Sell = loss; intentional friction |
| Meta upgrade: Start Gold | +15 / +35 / +60 | Applied at game start |
| Meta upgrade: Wave Bonus | +10% / +20% | Applied to wave completion bonus |

## Economy Pacing Targets

| Wave | Target Gold Available | Expected Tower Count |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 95–120g | 1–2 towers |
| 5 (mini-boss) | 200–300g | 3–4 towers |
| 10 (mini-boss) | 400–550g | 5–6 towers |
| 25 (final boss) | 800–1200g | 7–10 towers, multiple upgrades |

---

## Balance Levers

- **Kill gold too low:** Players can't place enough towers by wave 10 → increase kill gold for mid-tier enemies
- **Wave bonus too high:** Players can afford too many towers too quickly → reduce bonus multiplier or delay bonus timing
- **Sell value too high (> 70%):** Players sell and rebuild without cost → keep at 60%
- **Tower costs too uniform:** All towers feel the same → maintain a 2× cost spread between cheapest and most expensive

---

## Crystal Economy

Crystals are the premium meta currency. Design targets:
- A free-to-play player should fully upgrade one meta category after ~10 hours
- IAP crystals should accelerate progression, not gate content
- No gameplay-critical mechanic should require crystal spending
