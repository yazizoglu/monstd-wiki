# Damage Types Guide

Understanding the resistance system is the key skill that separates good players from great ones in MonsTD.

---

## The Matchup System

Every tower deals one damage type. Every enemy may have one or more resistances. Hitting a resistant enemy with its resisted damage type deals significantly reduced damage.

**Rule of thumb:** always have at least two different damage types active in your build.

## How to Read Enemy Waves

Before each wave, inspect incoming enemies:

- **See armored enemies?** → You need [[Lightning Tower]] — it deals 35% bonus damage through armor
- **See frost-immune enemies?** → [[Frost Tower]] damage is completely negated — use Fire upgrades
- **See flying enemies?** → Only [[Arrow Tower]] and [[Lightning Tower]] can target them
- **See regenerating enemies?** → [[Poison Tower]] DoT deals 50% bonus and outpaces healing
- **See poison-resistant?** → Avoid [[Poison Tower]] as primary — switch to Lightning or Frost

---

## Resistance vs Immunity

Some enemies have full immunity (−75% or more), not just resistance:

- **Fire Immune** (Lav Golem, Twin Dragon, Demon): fire upgrade paths deal almost nothing
- **Frost Immune** (all Map 3 bosses): Frost Tower does chip damage at best
- **Poison Resist** (Ent, Golem, Rat King): Poison Tower is effectively useless on these

## Build Diversity Rule

> At minimum, always have **Physical + one elemental type** active. By [[Level 2]], add a second elemental type.

| Level | Recommended Build |
|---|---|
| [[Level 1]] — Cursed Wilds | Arrow + Cannon + Lightning + Frost |
| [[Level 2]] — Volcanic Inferno | Arrow + Lightning + Frost + Poison |
| [[Level 3]] — Frozen Tundra | Arrow + Fire upgrades + Poison (avoid Frost damage) |
