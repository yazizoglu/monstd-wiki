# Damage Types Guide

Understanding the resistance system is the key skill that separates good players from great ones in MonsTD.

---

## The Matchup System

Every tower deals one damage type. Every enemy may have one or more resistances. Hitting a resistant enemy with its resisted damage type deals significantly reduced damage.

**Rule of thumb:** always have at least two different damage types active in your build.

## How to Read Enemy Waves

Before each wave, inspect incoming enemies:

- **See armored enemies?** → You need [[Lightning Tower]] or [[Poison Tower]]
- **See frost-resistant?** → Avoid relying on [[Frost Tower]] for damage
- **See flying enemies?** → Keep at least one [[Arrow Tower]] covering that section
- **See regenerating enemies?** → [[Poison Tower]] DoT outpaces their healing

---

## Resistance Doesn't Mean Immune

Resisted damage is reduced, not zero. A [[Frost Tower]] hitting a [[Skeleton]] still deals some damage — it just won't be efficient. If it's all you have, it still contributes.

---

## Build Diversity Rule

> At minimum, always have **Physical + one elemental type** active. By Level 2, add a second elemental type.

- Level 1: Arrow + Cannon + Frost is sufficient
- Level 2: Arrow + Lightning + Frost/Poison required
- Level 3: Arrow + Lightning + Poison; avoid Frost investment
